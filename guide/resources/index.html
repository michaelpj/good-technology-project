Career advice

  • 80,000 Hours provides advice on how to have a greater social impact with your career.

Startup advice

  • Y Combinator’s list of resources for startups.
  • Y Combinator’s class on how to start a startup.
  • A summary of Y Combinator’s key ideas on how to start a startup.
  • Zero to One by Peter Thiel, who cofounded Paypal and Palantir. The book is based on his Stanford lectures on startups.
  • The Lean Startup which describes how to test the assumptions behind a startup.
  • The Startup Owners Manual - a detailed manual on starting a startup, with step-by-step instructions.

Choosing a cause

  • 80,000 Hours on which cause is most effective.
  • Effective Altruism Ventures’ list of causes they’d like to see projects in.
  • The Open Philanthropy Project research where to donate for maximum impact. Though their research is focussed on giving, they do prioritisation research that is useful for anyone working out how to do good.
  • The Global Proirities Project does research on what are the world’s most pressing problems. They’ve published a flowchart to help people to decide what cause to work on.
  • Y Combinator’s request for startups lists areas that they’d like to see startups in that they think will make the world a better place.
  • What happened to the future? is a manifesto by Peter Thiel’s Founders Fund. It argues that venture capitalists should fund more transformational technologies and suggests some types of technology that they think are important.

The technology for good community

  • Tech for Good - news and community building in tech for good.
  • impact.tech - a community of technologists who want to do good, with events in San Francisco. Search meetup.com for ‘tech for good’.
  • London Tech For Good meetup.
  • Try searching meetup.com for ‘tech for good’ meetups in your area.
